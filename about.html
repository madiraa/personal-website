Who I Am

I’m Madison, a political scientist, data scientist, and organizer passionate about using data, technology, and collective action to improve the human condition. My work bridges the worlds of research, activism, and open-source intelligence (OSINT).

What I Do

  • Political Data Science & Analysis: Quantitative research, data-driven policy analysis, and machine learning for social science.
  • Political Organizing: Campaign strategy and implementation, grassroots mobilization, and digital advocacy for progressive causes.
  • Nonviolent Direct Action: Practiced in designing and leading nonviolent direct action for social and political change.
  • OSINT: Open-source intelligence collection and analysis for the defense of targeted communities.
  • Community Building: Facilitating collaboration, knowledge sharing, and mutual aid in diverse communities.

Values & Approach

  • Justice & Integrity: I have a seething hatred for the abuse of power and for injustice in all forms.
  • Equity & Inclusion: Committed to safe, affirming spaces for LGBTQ+ folks, people of color, and all marginalized people.
  • Empathy & Action: I pursue real-world impact guided by care for people.
  • Curiosity: Always learning, experimenting, exploring new disciplines, asking questions, and seeking answers.

Quick Facts

  • BA in Political Science (International Relations), minors in Public Policy and Dutch (fluent).
  • Voluntary leave from M.S. at Columbia University (due to cost); currently earning an A.S. in Data Science at SF City College.
  • Caregiver for adults with disabilities.
  • OSINTing with Open Shadows Intelligence, Research & Intervention Service (OSIRIS).
